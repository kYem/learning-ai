## UX & Design Practice (Smashing Magazine, in-window only)

- **UX Copy Ecosystems (Jürgen Zimmermann)** — *Apr 15* — Practical guide to setting up content design infrastructure: shared terminology, voice rules, contribution workflows. Useful companion if you're experimenting with AI-generated copy and need a governance layer.
- **Designing for Low Vision (Ian Lloyd)** — *Apr 14* — Contrast, zoom, and reflow patterns that go beyond WCAG minimums — practical considerations for the millions of users who have vision issues but aren't fully blind.
- **Top vs. Side Navigation (Taras Bakusevych)** — *Apr 13* — Decision framework for choosing navigation placement based on task frequency, content density, and responsive behavior. Good reference piece.
- **Designing for Neurodiversity (Stéphanie Walter)** — *Apr 10* — Guidelines for dyslexia, dyscalculia, autism, and ADHD. Cognitive-load reduction patterns that generalize well beyond accessibility.
- **UX Impact in Legacy Organizations** — *Apr 10* — Practical guidance for designers working inside companies with old systems and broken processes — how to drive measurable outcomes without a clean-slate rewrite.

## Key Takeaway

This was an unusually concentrated week for AI-design-tool launches: **Anthropic (Apr 17) and Canva (Apr 16) both announced major agentic design products within 24 hours of each other**, each aimed squarely at Figma's turf — Anthropic with a generate-visuals-from-prompts product, Canva with a conversational workflow that edits outputs in place and calls external tools. The more interesting signal for Kes's Starter-AI track: both products ship as *research previews* with usage-based scaling — meaning the reliability, rate-limiting, and uptime problems you'd be pitching in an Nscale-style interview are *exactly* the problems these teams are solving this week, in public. Worth skimming the Anthropic and Canva engineering/launch posts for concrete examples to reference.
